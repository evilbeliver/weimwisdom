If you walked into my backyard this week, you might think you'd accidentally stumbled into a five-star canine boutique hotel.

With a brand-new deck finally installed, I've been spending the last few days helping the duo get acclimated to the new sights, sounds, and textures of their upgraded kingdom. My goal was "comfort." Their goal? Full-blown luxury.

As you can see, it didn't take long. While I may have guided them into these poses on the loungers, Loki and Paige didn't hesitate for a second. They essentially looked at me and asked, "Great, so where are the num-nums and the umbrella drinks?"

## The "Off Switch" is Real

Looking at them now, you'd never guess their "day jobs." Take Paige, for instance. She's a high-drive working dog I've trained for hunt and field work. Just this morning, she was dialed in, running reps for shed antler hunting (her "On Switch"). But the moment she hits that deck? Total "Off Switch." She's currently practicing for her summer career as a professional baked potato.

Then we have Loki (aka Loki Bear, aka Tri-cera-hops, aka Mr. Poofy, aka... well, a few other names depending on his mood!). Loki is my mystery man. We don't know much about his life before us, other than he was a serial escape artist until his previous owners finally gave up. He's been a challenge, but man, do I love this guy.

## From Ear Piercings to Play Bows

It wasn't always lounging and sunshine. You know that a good training plan is the only thing keeping me from being their full-time butler.

Believe it or not, a few months ago, I couldn't even work these two in the same space. When we first brought Paige home 1.5 years ago, Loki hated her so much he actually punctured her ear on day one. He gave her a piercing but didn't even have the decency to buy her a diamond stud to go with it! We spent the first four weeks living behind baby gates.

Fast forward to today: I can walk them both on loose leashes with total confidence. But the real miracle happened this morning: They played. Not just a little—a full-on session initiated by two play bows from Paige. No hackles, no teeth, no "dad" intervention required. Just pure, goofy joy.

## Spoiled? Maybe. Happy? Definitely.

I'll be diving deeper into their individual journeys in future posts, but seeing them together on those loungers reminds me why the work matters.

Yes, my dogs are spoiled rotten. I won't even argue that! But that "spoiled" life is built on a foundation of effort, time, and a whole lot of love. It's about making sure they feel safe, secure, and mentally fulfilled enough to finally trade in their anxieties for a nap in the sun.

Stay tuned for more updates from the "resort"—I have a feeling the service requirements from the guests are only going to go up from here!
