I can't emphasize enough how awesome it is to finally be at this stage. Especially with summer approaching, I'm ready to stop vibrating at the same frequency as my dogs.

If you've been following along, you know that when we first brought Paige home, her primary goal in life was to physically outrun the reality of her new situation. Looking back at my Fitbit data is like looking at a crime scene. I can tell you the exact day she arrived - Saturday, August 10th, 2024 - because my step count hit 16.5K and basically never looked back.

I had weeks where I was averaging 23K steps. That's 11.2 miles a day. Did I mention somewhere on this site that Weims have high energy? Because I meant "they have enough energy to power a small midwestern city."

The real kicker? Those were just my steps. Paige actually walked my daughter right into a medical boot. My kid got shin splints just trying to help me keep up with the Loki-Paige duo.

Yeah... we're a high-performance household. Or a broken one. Take your pick.

When we brought her home in 2024, I went from a respectable sub-10K average to a grueling 12,668-step average over five months. I was literally pounding out 17K to 20K steps a day just to try and tire her out. Spoiler: It didn't work, but it did take a toll. I lost 30+ lbs during the "Paige Relocation Tour."

It's the most expensive and exhausting weight loss plan on the market.

2025 didn't start much better. Even though the average settled at 12K, I was still walking two dogs individually because, well, sanity.

But finally? Relief.

Being able to exercise both of these "heathens" together has been a literal life-saver for my joints. Even during hunting season, where I'm doing 20K days chasing birds and rabbits, my overall average this year is just under 11K. I never thought I'd be the guy saying I'm "happy" to be doing only five miles a day, but here we are.

The morning routine has shifted from a frantic "get her out before she explodes" sprint to a somewhat civil tethered walk. Are they both tethered to my waist? Yes. Is that smart or incredibly stupid? Depends if we see Loki's nemesis, the Boxer.

We've graduated from "let's just make it to the porch" to "let's walk the neighborhood and beyond." This morning, we did 2.6 miles together, and - miracle of miracles - they actually crashed afterward. Now, they spend their time baking in the sun on their new deck, which apparently is the only thing more effective at draining their batteries than an ultramarathon.

My knees are currently sending me a thank-you note. While these two definitely helped me get into shape, I think we've all earned a break. Plus, it's much easier to film Loki's ears flopping when I'm not gasping for air or exhausted.
