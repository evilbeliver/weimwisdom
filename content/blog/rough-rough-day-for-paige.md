# Rough Rough Day for Paige: The Factory Reset Button Has Been Located

They say a dog is man's best friend. But they don't usually mention that your "best friend" sometimes wakes up, looks in the mirror, and decides to undo two years of therapy and training in a single morning.

Yesterday, I did something bold. Something brave. I went into the woods to chase turkeys by myself. No dogs. Just me, the trees, and the blissful silence that only exists when you aren't being stalked by two Velcro-dogs with zero respect for personal space. I figured, "Hey, these two A-Hats (they earned it) have come so far. They love the wife, they love the daughter. We're in a good place."

Wrong. So very wrong.

## The Metal Leash Returns

I walked through the front door, still smelling like the woods, and there it was. Sitting on the counter like a haunting relic from a darker time: The Metal Leash.

We haven't touched that thing in over eighteen months. For those of you new to the Weim life, we had to buy the "indestructible" version because Paige used to treat nylon leashes like a delicious five-piece nugget meal. Seeing it on the counter is the equivalent of seeing a "Caution: Radioactive" sign on your fridge.

Apparently, when Paige woke up and realized "Dad" wasn't there to cater to her every whim, she decided to hit the Factory Reset Button.

It started with Bumper Toss—her absolute favorite game. My daughter tried to play, Paige decided she didn't have to listen to "the help," and promptly lost her bumper privileges. Did she take that sitting down? Of course not.

Instead, this 90-pound ball of silver entitlement decided to challenge Loki for the crown of the house. When my daughter tried to step in, Paige actually had the audacity to snap and growl. The nerve.

Luckily, my daughter knows the drill. She took charge, the metal leash came out, and Paige spent the afternoon learning that her "rights" are actually "privileges" that can be revoked faster than you can say silver ghost.

## The Big Sigh of Relief

I'll be honest: I was prepared for a long, miserable night. I took them both out to the school later that evening, fully expecting to have to play the "Strict Parent" role all night. But as soon as we hit the grass? Compliance. Perfect recalls. Not a single "A-Hat" moment to be found.

It was like the "Reset" finally finished downloading and she remembered, "Oh right, I'm a dog, not the CEO of this family."

Weims are basically toddlers in expensive fur coats. One minute they're the light of your life, and the next, you're wondering if you can trade them for a pet rock. But after a long sleep, we're back to our routine. They aren't perfect—I'd be a liar if I said they were—but they're ours.

## Hang in There, Fellow Weim Parents

To any fellow Weim parents out there currently staring at a chewed-up leash or a defiant 90-pound shadow: Hang in there. The "Mostly Good Dog" version usually comes back after a nap.
