## DRIVE mode: Activated. CHOMP mode: Reactivated.

Someone definitely woke up on the farm side of the bed today.

Paige pulled a total "Uno Reverse" card on me this morning. We've worked incredibly hard to get the farm out of the girl, but let's be honest: that's a losing battle. This morning, she was vibrating with so much drive she was barking just to get the Earth to rotate faster.

Now, we don't give into her temper tantrums—even if her "protest" style can be a bit… destructive. We're well past the days of accidental bite marks and communication breakdowns; I can read her moods like a book now. And this morning's chapter was titled: "I Am Going Hunting and No One Can Stop Me."

## The Great Kennel Detonation

The second I released her from her kennel (yes, she sleeps in the "indoor dog cave" in our bedroom), she didn't walk out—she detonated. She busted out of there like there was a prize waiting in the hallway.

The problem? It's not upland season any more, and It's turkey season. Which means we can't even hit the public lands to let her burn off that "zoomie" fuel off-leash.

Since I wasn't about to let her redecorate the house, I had to pivot. Game on.

## Five-Antler Challenge

I upped the ante. Instead of our usual two-antler "warm-up," I salted the yard and the woods behind the house with five different sheds. It turned into a high-stakes 15-minute session of nose and leg work.

Did she nail the technicals? Not quite. But she found every single one. We've only been at this a few weeks, but she's finally realizing that this isn't a "one and done" game. It's a "keep hunting" game, and the rewards are starting to click.

That session took the edge off, but I can tell: it's going to be one of those days. I'm pairing a quick walk with that play session and hoping the 80-degree sun does the rest. If I'm lucky, I might actually get her to "Baking Potato" stage by noon.

## The Weimaraner Energy Tax

Have I mentioned the energy these silver balls of fur have? They don't just keep you moving—they keep your brain on a permanent treadmill.
