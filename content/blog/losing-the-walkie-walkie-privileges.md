You know those days when you feel like you've finally mastered the art of being a "Good Boy"? Loki Dog was on a roll. For weeks, we've been nailing our neighborhood strolls. We had a system: a "challenge" appears, Loki and Paige both sit, I handle the protection detail, they do well and they get rewarded for it, It was a beautiful, synchronized dance of Weimaraner grace.

Until this morning.

## Enter: The Boxer

I don't know what this specific Boxer did in a past life—maybe he stole Loki's favorite dream-squirrel?—but he is Loki's ultimate nemesis. This morning, we were still on our own driveway, safely tucked away from the street, when the Nemesis appeared.

Loki didn't just bark. He went Full Poof.

If you've seen Shark Tale, imagine Mr. Sykes when he's angry. Loki's hackles didn't just rise; his entire body turned into a pufferfish of rage. He hit the end of that leash like he was trying to launch himself into orbit.

I tried the redirects. I tried the "Look at me!" prompts. I tried every trick in the book. But Loki had entered the "No-Service Zone." When he realized he couldn't get to the Boxer, he decided to redirect his spicy attitude toward poor Paige.

## Big mistake, Mr. Poofy Pants

Dad was fully prepared. The tantrum resulted in an immediate revocation of "Walkie-Walkie Privileges." Loki was escorted back inside to think about his life choices, while Paige and I enjoyed a peaceful (and significantly less poofy) stroll.

Here's hoping the "Pufferfish" deflates by this evening so we can try again. Cross your fingers for us!
