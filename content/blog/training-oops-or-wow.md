With the temperatures climbing here in the south, I traded our usual walk for a backyard training session. Since we’ve shifted to training Paige and Loki together, I’ve been using a lot of "Place" bed work. My goal? Solidifying individual commands by name so I can actually control the chaos when both are in the mix.

We have been working on this for a few weeks now indoors, and now I had them both on their mats on the deck while I headed down the stairs. The challenge: individual releases with outdoor distractions (birds, squirrels, butterflies, heck a blade of grass looking at Loki wrong..).

## The Wow Moment:

On this day, they nailed it. I released Paige, sent her back to "Place," and she ran right up the stairs to her “Place” bed. Loki stayed glued to my side until I gave him the green light. "Loki, OK! Place!" and off he went, charging up the stairs.

## The "Oops" (or the Win?):

I walked up the stairs to hand out the rewards, expecting to see these two on their respective mats. Instead, I found a total takeover.

Loki decided that "Place" actually meant "squeeze onto Paige’s bed and breathe down her neck." He had this massive, goofy grin on his face—he knew exactly what he was doing. Poor Paige looked like she was enduring a trial by fire. You could see it in her eyes: “Dad he has cooties. Why is he touching me?”

I couldn't help it; I started laughing like crazy. The real "Wow" was that Paige actually held her command despite the personal space violation. After five minutes of forced snuggling, I released her to the other bed and gave them both a jackpot reward.

Watching them go from mortal enemies to a pack that can (mostly) share a zip code is a huge win. Even if Loki is still a world-class pest, I Love that guy!
