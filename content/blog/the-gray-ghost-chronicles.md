# Welcome to the first of many posts 

Who am I.. no one really, just someone with a passion for punishiment and a lot of lived expereriences I guess, as it comes with the love of this breed! I created this site and blog to give back more to the Weim community and help others with these beutiful heathens. You'll probably see me refer to them as a..hats from time to time… don’t judge me; if you’ve lived with them for 25+ years like I have, you know they’ve earned the title. So follow along if you would like on our journies"

## 1. Taffy: Where it all Began

 My journey started over 40 years ago with a Weim named Taffy. I can still see those giant soft ears in my head. She was my constant companion, even when she was busy stealing my blankets in the middle of the night because she was cold. Before she left us, I made her a childhood promise that I’d find her again one day. I didn't fully understand what that meant then, but looking back, I think I’ve spent my life keeping that promise.

## 2. Tanna: The Queen of the Kitchen

In 2005, I convinced my wife to adopt a five-year-old puppy mill survivor named Tanna (aka "Sniffy"). She was meant to be a companion for our neurotic red Golden Retriever, Sandy.
Tanna was a project—she survived two rounds of heartworm treatment, which is no small feat when you're trying to keep a "gray ghost" on a leash-only exercise regimen. She was also a genius. My wife’s introduction to the breed involved "trashcan wars" and watching Tanna learn to open doors, cabinets, the pantry, the fridge, and even the oven. She and Sandy became inseparable until we lost Sandy to Megaesophagus.

## 3. Belle: Enter the "Raptor"

Seeing Tanna’s heartbreak after Sandy passed broke us. A few months later, we went to look at a puppy. We didn’t pick Belle; she picked us by deciding to eat my daughter’s hair.
Tanna wasn't thrilled about the "little raptor" at first, but they spent a year and a half together before Tanna passed at age 14. Belle eventually became the matriarch of our family—our "Dr. Penguin" (shoutout to the American Dad fans). Losing her in September 2023 was a hit that leveled me. I couldn’t even look at another Weim without crying.

## 4. Loki and Paige: The Current Crew

The road eventually led us to South Carolina and a local rescue where we became "foster fails" with Loki.
Loki is, without a doubt, the biggest "a..hat" we’ve ever had. He came with many fear aggressions, and in 2023, he lost a front leg to cancer. They gave him six months; but three years later, our "Tri-ceri-hops" is still going like crazy. He has a will to live unlike any dog I’ve ever met.
Then along came Paige. In August 2024, a friend told me about a 3-year-old Weim on a farm who needed a better life. I wasn't sure if my heart was ready, but she melted me instantly. Bringing her home wasn't easy, and we are still navigating the dynamics between her and Loki 1.5 years later—but that’s a story for another post.

## 5. What’s Next?

As of today, we are living with two soul-sucking Gray Ghosts. Life is loud, messy, and full of attitude, and I wouldn’t have it any other way. I look forward to sharing more of our journey with you as Loki and Paige find new ways to get into trouble.

## 6. The Bottom Line: 

Weimaraners aren't for everyone. They require time, energy, patience, and commitment. But if you're up for the challenge, you'll gain a best friend who thinks you hung the moon and will follow you anywhere (and I do mean anywhere - including the bathroom).
