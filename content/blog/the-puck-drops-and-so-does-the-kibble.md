## Game Day is finally here!

In our house, we are a hockey family through and through. Now, to be fair, none of us have ever actually laced up skates, but we have mastered the art of the "enthusiastic living room cheer" (which usually sounds like a chorus of screams to the dogs).

As many of you know, our household is a divided locker room. It's a constant battle for hockey supremacy:

* **The Wife:** A die-hard Chicago Blackhawks fan.
* **The Oldest Son:** A Tampa Bay Lightning fan (we're still waiting on the DNA results for that one).
* **The Rest of Us:** Proudly bleeding Orange and Black for the Philadelphia Flyers!

We've had some legendary clashes over the years. My wife's team has historically gotten the better of us in the Finals—a wound that still stings more than a slap shot to the shin. But today? Today is different. It's been six long years, but the Flyers are back in the playoffs!

Paige and Loki have been channeling the Broad Street Bullies of the past and present. They've been rocking their jerseys and practicing their "game faces" (which mostly look like they're begging for popcorn).

## 🏒 Official Hockey Etiquette for Dogs (by Paige & Loki)

To keep things "civil" during the playoffs, we've established a few ground rules for the pups:

1. **The Hip Check Rule:** Just because there are no boards in the living room doesn't mean the checking stops. Paige has mastered a world-class hip check that she regularly uses to send Loki sliding across the hardwood.

2. **No High-Sticking:** This includes tails. If a goal is scored, tail-wagging must be controlled to avoid knocking over any "beers or gear" on the coffee table.

3. **The "Biscuit" Policy:** In hockey, the "biscuit" is the puck. In this house, if any actual biscuits (or chips) hit the floor, they are considered live play and belong to the first dog to reach them.

4. **Penalty Box:** Intrusive sniffing or blocking the TV during a power play will result in a 2-minute minor in the "Penalty Box" (the dog bed).

5. **Respect the Sweater:** Jerseys stay on until the final horn, even if Paige thinks hers makes her look a bit "over-indexed" on defense.

Things are going to get rowdy tonight. I just hope Paige is ready for the high-decibel yelling that happens every time the puck crosses the blue line. Loki seems ready to hit the ice, or at least clean up any chips that fall during the power play.

Let the rivalry begin, let the house be loud, and for the love of all things holy…

**GO FLYERS! 🧡🖤🐕**
