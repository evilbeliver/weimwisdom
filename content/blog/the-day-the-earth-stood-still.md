If you own a Weimaraner, you know the drill. It's 10% majestic hunting dog and 90% unhinged gray raptor. We live in a world where "personal space" is a myth and "quiet" usually means someone is doing something they are NOT supposed to be doing.. 

So, with my mother-in-law visiting, I prepared for the worst. I expected a demolition derby. What I got instead was… competence?

## Phase 1: The Walker of Doom

We started prepping a week early by introducing a walker. Now, to a Weimaraner, a walker looks like a giant, four-legged metal spider designed specifically to steal their joy. At first, Paige looked at it like it was an alien spacecraft. By Friday, she was treating it with the same indifference she treats my requests to get off the sofa. Win #1.

## Phase 2: The Coup d'État

Then came the actual arrival. My wife returns after a week away, a guest enters the house, and the dogs—Loki and Paige—decide it's time to achieve terminal velocity.

In a moment of pure desperation/genius, I handed the leads to my daughter. I figured, Hey, if they're going to knock someone over, let it be the youth. They bounce.

But then, the unthinkable happened.

She dropped the "Place" beds. She gave the command. And these two absolute a-hats… listened. They didn't just listen; they practically teleported to their beds. Loki tried one tactical breakout toward the garage, but my daughter shut him down like a casino pit boss. He slinked back to his bed, probably wondering where his life went wrong.

## Phase 3: The Snack Test (The Ultimate Boss Fight)

The real test came when my mother-in-law sat down in the family room with a snack. Normally, Paige views a guest with food as a vending machine that just needs to be nudged hard enough to drop the goods. She is a master manipulator. She has a PhD in "Sad Puppy Eyes."

But there she stayed. Glued to the bed.

My daughter was running the show like a pro, rewarding the good behavior and ignoring the "please-sir-I'm-starving" theatrics. I just stood there, never had to take over, never had to help my daughter. It took 20 minutes of "Place" before they finally reached a manageable level of excitement, at which point they received a treat jackpot that would make a Vegas high-roller blush.

## The Verdict

No one got knocked over. No one fought over who was the favorite grandpuppy. For one glorious Day 1, my house didn't feel like a chaotic zoo—it felt like a home with trained animals.

I know it's only the first 24 hours, but I'm calling it. They nailed it. My daughter did amazing, and I'm happy sometimes to just be the guy who pays for the kibble.
