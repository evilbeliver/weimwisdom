I've been brushing up on my D&D lore lately—been a while, and I realized I don't have dogs; I have Shadow Demons.

According to the manual, these creatures excel at stealth, manipulation, and are expert Assassins. If that isn't a Weimaraner in a nutshell, I don't know what is.

## Stealth & Manifestation

I can be in the kitchen with no dog in sight for miles. But the second a lid clicks or a bag crinkles? They manifest out of thin air. Suddenly, Paige and Loki are just there, staring at me with the hollow, haunting eyes of creatures who haven't eaten in centuries. It's a masterful illusion—mostly because I just fed them twenty minutes ago.

## The Art of Manipulation

They are tactical geniuses. They scout the family for weaknesses and exploit them ruthlessly. They know exactly who will succumb to the "starving orphan" look and who holds the line. Now, they're preparing for a new target: my mother-in-law is visiting this week.

It'll be Paige's first time meeting her, and we're currently in "Pre-Boss Battle" training. Since my mother-in-law uses a walker and a wheelchair, we're busy desensitizing the demons to heavy machinery in confined spaces.

## The Training Montage

I'm currently hunting down a wheelchair and walker to stage around the house. If I'm being honest, there is a 100% chance this devolves into "Wheelchair Races: Weim Edition" by Wednesday. I'm just hoping Paige learns that the wheelchair is a "no-go zone" and not a mobile chariot for her to hijack.

## The Assassins

As for the "Assassin" trait? If it breathes and enters our backyard, it's being hunted. I should probably start hiring them out as professional pest control to pay for their kibble.

Wish us luck this week. If you see a gray blur speeding past on a wheelchair, you'll know the Shadow Demons have officially taken over.
